PLAYER: TJ DeFalco
POSITION: Opposite Hitter
NATIONALITY: American
CLUBS: Sir Safety Perugia (Italy), Long Beach State (NCAA)
LESSONS: 10

=== MINDSET ===
The opposite hitter is the closer — they exist to put the ball away in the clutch. DeFalco trains a "no fear" kill mentality: when the game is tight, the opposite does not tip or roll, they attack full swing at the open court. He uses visualization sessions before matches, mentally running through his attack angles against each blocker he expects to face.

=== ATTACKING FROM THE RIGHT SIDE ===
The right-side approach is a 4-step mirror of the outside approach. The final jump should position the hitter slightly inside the antenna — not behind it. Contact the ball above and slightly behind the hitting shoulder for a cross-court angle. For a line shot from the right side, the arm swing comes through the ball toward the left sideline; the body opens slightly left at contact.

=== PIPE ATTACK ===
The pipe (back-row attack from position 6) is DeFalco's secondary weapon. Approach angle: diagonal toward the net, not straight. Jump distance should land the hitter between positions 2 and 3 of the 3-meter line. Arm swing direction is straight down into the deep corners. Contact height must be above the net or the attack angle becomes too flat.

=== BLOCKING AS AN OPPOSITE ===
The opposite blocker's primary responsibility is the outside hitter on the opponent's left side. Key: penetrate before the hitter contacts the ball. DeFalco drills a "late hands" technique — keeping hands pulled back until the opponent's arm swing commits, then thrusting forward to close the angle.

=== PHYSICAL TRAINING ===
DeFalco emphasizes plyometric work: depth jumps, single-leg box jumps, and lateral bounding to develop reactive strength. He trains approach footwork with resistance bands around the ankles to build drive strength in the final two steps. Jump training is always done before hitting reps — never after — because fatigue changes mechanics.

=== SERVE ===
Jump topspin serve from the right side of the court targeting the seam between the libero and the right-back defender (zone 1 deep corner). The toss is consistent — DeFalco tosses to the same spot every serve to prevent the serve from telegraphing the target. Varies speed, not position.

=== KEY QUOTES ===
"Tipping in a big moment is an apology. I never apologize on the court."
"Train your approach until it runs itself. Your brain should be reading the block, not your feet."
"The block is information. Use it, do not fear it."
