PLAYER: Antoine Brizard
POSITION: Setter
NATIONALITY: French
CLUBS: Chaumont VB 52 (France), Berlin Recycling Volleys (Germany)
LESSONS: 9

=== MINDSET ===
The setter is the quarterback — the architect of every rally. Brizard trains decision-making speed above all else: the correct decision delivered slowly is the wrong decision. Under pressure, the setter must appear calm even when reading chaos. He studies blockers and defenders constantly during warmup to catalog tendencies before the first rally.

=== HAND SETTING TECHNIQUE ===
Contact point is slightly above and in front of the forehead — not behind the head. All ten fingers contact the ball simultaneously. The wrists are the last hinge before release; stiff wrists produce floaters, loose wrists produce tight sets. The set trajectory for a quick set (1-tempo) is nearly flat — no arc. For a high ball, the trajectory peaks at 3-4 meters above the net.

=== READING THE BLOCK ===
Brizard watches the middle blocker's feet, not their hands. Feet pointing toward the outside hitter means the middle is committing early — go to the opposite or run the pipe. Feet square to the net means the middle is holding — run the quick or the combination. He identifies the weakest blocker in the first two rotations and targets them repeatedly.

=== BACK SET ===
The release angle of the back set is determined by the elbow flare. Right elbow drops, set goes right. He practices the back set blindfolded to develop pure muscle memory — the eyes can lie, the wrists cannot.

=== SETTING ON THE RUN ===
When the pass is off the net, platform-set over hand-set. Brizard trains the platform set to be indistinguishable in trajectory from a hand set so the blockers cannot identify the hitter early. Footwork: always approach the ball from the right side so the chest opens toward the antenna on release.

=== DUMP TECHNIQUE ===
The setter dump must be disguised as a set until the last 0.2 seconds. Normal set posture, normal approach to the ball — then at contact, redirect with the right hand. Target: the 2-zone corner between the right back defender and the right antenna. Best used on the second touch after a clean pass when the opposite blocker is not watching.

=== KEY QUOTES ===
"A setter who surprises their own team is a bad setter. Surprise the other team, never your own."
"I watch film on blockers like a chess player studies openings. Every pattern has a counter."
"The best set is the one the hitter asked for before you even touched the ball."
