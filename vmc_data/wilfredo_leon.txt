PLAYER: Wilfredo León
POSITION: Outside Hitter
NATIONALITY: Cuban-Italian
CLUBS: Sir Safety Perugia (Italy), previous Cuba national team, Poland national team
LESSONS: 11

=== MINDSET ===
Trains a "hunter mentality" — always seeking the ball. Mental reset protocol after errors: one deep breath, finger snap, immediate refocus on the next point. Outside hitters must radiate confidence to serve as the emotional anchor of the team. León emphasizes that mental resilience is trained, not innate — he repeats a personal mantra before every serve.

=== APPROACH AND ATTACKING ===
4-step approach. First two steps are deliberate reading steps: scan the setter's hands early and calculate the trajectory of the set before it leaves the setter's hands. Steps 3 and 4 are explosive acceleration into the jump. The arm swing is a full shoulder rotation — not a forearm snap. Contact point should be as high as possible, slightly in front of the hitting shoulder. Always attack the seam between the block and the sideline on the first tempo. On the second contact, look for the open court behind the block.

=== JUMP SERVE ===
León's signature weapon. The approach is three steps with a long final stride to generate horizontal momentum converted to vertical. The toss is 2-3 meters in front of the body — not above the head. Contacts the ball with maximum tension at the top of the swing. Targets the 1-zone and 5-zone corners. Serve pressure is the first line of offense: the goal is to pull the libero wide and create a poor first touch.

=== PASSING ===
Platform angle is set before the ball crosses the net — do not adjust mid-flight. Body weight transfers forward into the pass, never backward. In serve receive, León prefers a slightly open stance to cover more court. Communication with the libero is constant — call every ball.

=== BLOCKING ===
Outside hitters block cross-court as a default. Penetrate the net with hands, not elbows. Watch the opposite hitter's shoulder — it telegraphs the line shot 80% of the time. Seal the line on the setter dump when the setter's hips open toward the net.

=== KEY QUOTES ===
"The ball doesn't know if you're having a bad day. You must be the same player every single point."
"I practice the jump serve 200 times every session. What feels like genius is just repetition."
"An outside hitter who cannot pass is not an outside hitter — they are a liability."
