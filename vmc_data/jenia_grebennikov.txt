PLAYER: Jenia Grebennikov
POSITION: Libero
NATIONALITY: French (born Russia)
CLUBS: Chaumont VB 52, Modena Volley, Paris Saint-Georges
LESSONS: 8

=== MINDSET ===
The libero is the last line of defense and the emotional reset button of the team. Grebennikov treats every dig as a gift to the setter — not just a save. He trains to make the impossible look routine so hitters trust the dig will arrive in a hittable position. The libero must have a short memory: a missed dig is forgotten before the next rally begins.

=== SERVE RECEIVE POSITIONING ===
Starting position is based on the server's toss, not the server's approach. The toss reveals the serve target 0.3 seconds earlier than the swing. Grebennikov positions himself slightly off-center to the server's strong side. He reads jump floaters differently from jump serves: the float requires a later platform commitment because the ball movement is unpredictable until the last meter.

=== PLATFORM MECHANICS ===
Straight arms — no bend at the wrists during contact. The platform is a single rigid surface. Hip rotation (not arm swing) generates the angle of the rebound. For balls hit directly at the body, drop the platform flat and let hip extension drive the ball upward. Grebennikov practices the "no-arm" drill: receiving the ball with hands clasped behind the back to train pure platform angle from body positioning.

=== EMERGENCY DIGS ===
One-arm digs: lead with the shoulder, not the elbow. The elbow leads to shanks; the shoulder leads to redirected saves. For the sprawl, Grebennikov plants the outside foot and slides laterally — never diving forward, which takes him out of position for the next play. The pancake: used for balls less than 15cm off the floor. Hand flat, palm down, slide it under the ball at the moment of contact.

=== SERVE ===
Liberos cannot serve in most international rules, but Grebennikov trains a float serve for beach and club contexts: minimal arm swing, contact through the middle-bottom of the ball, zero wrist snap. The float generates movement by the absence of spin, not by applying curve.

=== COMMUNICATION ===
Grebennikov calls every ball loudly — "mine," "out," "yours" — with no hesitation. Hesitation creates collision. He calls balls out before they land only when he has seen the angle establish at least 3 meters before impact.

=== KEY QUOTES ===
"I do not save the ball. I give the setter a perfect second touch every time. That is my only job."
"Liberos who dive for everything look exciting. Liberos who never need to dive win championships."
"Your feet must already be moving before your brain decides where to go."
